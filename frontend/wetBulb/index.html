Check your current wet-bulb temperature

Use this service to check whether you're at risk of overheating wherever you are.

35°C (95°F) wet-bulb temperature is extremely dangerous to humans!
wet-bulb temperatures from 31°C (88°F) start to get harmful.

Explanation: The wet-bulb temperature is the lowest temperature a thermometer shows that is covered in soaked cloth with air passing over it.

At such it is an ideal indicator of how cool a human can get by sweating. Higher temperature and higher relative humidity will cause sweating to become less effective up to a point where the human body can not cool down enough through natural ways and the climate becomes harmful.

This condition is reached at a wet-bulb temperature of 31°C (88°F) and above.

This tool uses the Stull formula that approximates the wet-bulb temperature well from −20° to 50°C and 5% to 99% relative humidity.
